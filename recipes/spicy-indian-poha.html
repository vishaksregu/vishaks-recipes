Spicy Indian Poha

Poha has long been one of my staple, go-to breakfast recipes ever since I started living on my own. In fact, it was the breakfast I looked forward to the most even in Uni

What I like the most about is that it is light, tasty, healthy and filling. And totally customizable

You can make it with potatoes, without potatoes, with veggies, without veggies, with nuts, without nuts. You can have it with bhujia, lime, gupshup peanuts or any combination of the three

Ingredients

  • Poha - 1 Cup
  • Onion - 1⁄2 finely chopped
  • Green Chili - 1 Chopped
  • Salt & Sugar - - a Pinch each
  • Roasted Peanuts (preferable salted) - a handful
  • Turmeric Powder - 1 Tsp
  • Oil - 1 Tsp

Instructions

  1. First wash, rinse and drain the Poha. Do it 2 - 3 times till the water is clear. Once done, drain out the water and sprinkle a pinch of salt and sugar. Mix well and leave it covered
  2. In an thick bottomed pan, heat some oil. Once hot, add the chopped onions and green chili, and sauté well
  3. Once the raw smell goes away, add peanuts and continue sauté well for 1 minute on a medium flame
  4. Add the turmeric and sauté for a minute. Now, lower the flame and add the drained poha. Mix well
  5. Spinkle and little water, and cook covered for a minute. Turn off the heat, and let it sit for another minute before serving
  6. Serve with Bhujia, a wedge of lemon (optional) and some gupshup peanuts
