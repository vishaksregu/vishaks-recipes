Classic Upma

Upma is another of those recipes that I did not really appreciate for a long long time

Growing up, upma was always a sweet breakfast. To be had with banana and sugar. And I never really got it

But living alone, I've started making a savoury version of Upma that was more to my liking. And here is the recipe

Ingredients

  • Roasted Sooji - 1 Cup
  • Onion - 1⁄2 Cup (chopped)
  • Carrot - 1 (Optional)
  • Cahsews - 6 (Optional)
  • Oil - 1 Tsp
  • Ginger - 1 Inch
  • Salt - 1 Pinch
  • Green Chili - 1 Chopped
  • Ghee - 1 Tsp
  • Water - 13⁄4 Cp

Instructions

  1. In a heavy bottomed pan, roast the sooji on a low flame till you get a nice aroma. Once done, set it aside
  2. In the same pan, heat some oil. Sauté onion, ginger and green chili for 3 minutes till the raw smell goes away
  3. Add the vegetables and cashews now, and sauté for 2 more minutes
  4. Add water and salt and bring to a rolling boil. The water must taste a little salty
  5. Slowly mix in the Sooji to the upma so as to not form lumps. Cook covered for 5 mins
  6. Add the ghee and mix well. Turn off the heat and let it rest covered for 10 more minutes before serving
